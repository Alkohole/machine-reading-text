also do you mean not present herself as in not telling them quicker?

No, more along the lines of standing before them in a towel. That's not the typical look Nightmare Moon goes with, after all. That alone should have been a dead giveaway that they weren't actually in the presence of the Nightmare Moon they are familiar with.

As for the gender thing, since Alex seems strangely comfortable as a female, I would put it into question whether Alex was a male, to begin with. Sure, physically one might appear so, but mentally is another thing. I should know, having gone through such a revelation early on in my life. Gender dysphoria isn't a surefire sign that something might not be as it should be, the comfortableness of being/appearing a certain way is. There is a lot more to it than that, but that's pretty much the basics.
